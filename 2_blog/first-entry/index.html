DAY 1

There was the list of free sources in the offer to start learning HTML and CSS, so I got registered to Udacity and started an HTML course.

I have never been a programmer, though I was working on the online project for 9 years. Sure, there were lots of things which were not that new to me - I know quite a lot about tags and all this stuff that always remains behind the screen. I also know a lot about the colours combinations and colours pallets, as I am familiar with Adobe Photoshop creative tools.

Moreover, my husband and his twin brother are IT specialists. They do lots of things together not only for work but also for fun and because they just can. I am proud of them both as they do even 3D games, just just have a look.

So all these terms like CSS, an absolute / a relative link, you-forget-to-close-the-tag and many other others are in my head.

Sure, Udacity helped me a lot to systematize my knowledge so I wrote my first code.

And I was really happy when I managed to code a button. The same day I learned how to see the results of my work on a webpage.

Udacity suggested coders would use ATOM platform, so I installed the platform and under their guidance I systematized my folder on the laptop with their platform. Now, every time I add a file to the folder code_it I can see it on Atom as well. Quite a miracle in a way, don't you think?
